Swift Proton VPN Extension

Privacy Policy

This page explains how the Swift Proton VPN Browser Extension and this GitHub Pages site handle information. We keep it short, clear, and practical.

Effective date: 2026-01-17

Quick Summary

We do not sell your data.

The extension stores your preferences locally in your browser.

The extension connects to Proton VPN services to authenticate and establish VPN connections, which may process data under Proton's own policies.

If you only need the short version, the three points above are the essence of the policy.

On This Site

GitHub Pages Data

This site is static and does not include analytics, tracking scripts, or cookies.

  • No account creation or sign-in.
  • No form submissions or data uploads.
  • Basic server logs may be collected by GitHub as part of hosting.

In The Extension

Information Processed

  • Connection preferences such as chosen servers, countries, and rules.
  • Authentication tokens required to access Proton VPN services.
  • Optional favorites or rule lists saved in your browser storage.

These items are stored locally in your browser unless you export them yourself.

Use And Sharing

How Data Is Used

  • To enable VPN connections and apply your site-based rules.
  • To display your saved preferences inside the extension UI.
  • To communicate with Proton VPN infrastructure for authentication and connection management.

This fork does not add extra telemetry or advertising SDKs beyond what Proton VPN services require to function.

Retention

How Long We Keep Data

Local extension data stays in your browser until you remove it or uninstall the extension. Exported files remain on your device until you delete them.

Security

How We Protect Data

We rely on browser storage protections and Proton VPN's secure APIs. Keep your browser updated and avoid installing untrusted extensions.

Changes

Policy Updates

If this policy changes, the effective date will be updated. Material changes will be summarized here.
